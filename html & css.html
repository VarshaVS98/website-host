Geospatial Data Portal to Store, Visualize, and Share the Laboratory Test Results

The Kerala Highway Research Institute (KHRI), working under the Kerala Public Works Department (PWD), has long been at the forefront of pioneering research and innovation within Kerala's infrastructure sector. The first step in infrastructure planning is to understand the soil conditions of the area. Using existing soil data from a nearby project can significantly save time and costs by eliminating the need for a new investigation. Since soil properties in neighboring areas are likely to be similar, this data provides a reliable baseline for understanding the general conditions, allowing the planning phase to begin faster. Additionally, knowing that the soil conditions are comparable helps standardize construction practices, reducing variability in methods and simplifying overall project management.

To address this, the Kerala Highway Research Institute (KHRI), under PWD, proposes creating a Geodatabase to store authenticated soil data using Google Earth and open-source software QGIS. This system will streamline the collection, organization, and visualization of soil data from borehole samples, laboratory tests, and borelog images. By linking geotagged data to specific geographic locations, this Geodatabase will reduce redundancy and provide stakeholders with easy access to reliable, spatially-referenced soil data. This approach will enhance decision-making for road, building, and bridge projects, ensuring more efficient and effective management of soil investigation data.

Laboratory Test

The samples collected under the supervision of the Kerala Highway Research Institute (KHRI) are tested in the KHRI’s laboratory to assess their index and engineering properties, and the soil are classified based on their properties as per IS 1498/ASTM D 2487. The index property of soil gives the information to identify and classify the soil, based on their physical characteristics, whereas, the engineering properties of soil determines the behaviour of soil under specific loading and environmental conditions. Assessmnet of the engineering property includes the determination of shear strength parameters, permeability characteristics, consolidation properties, dynamic behaviour, etc. The laboratory test procedure as per the Indian Standard conducted on the soil are as follows;

Sl. No. Laboratory Tests Code
1 Preparation of dry soil samples for various tests IS 2720 Part 1 : 1983

Preparation of dry soil samples:It involves air-drying, oven-drying, or using a desiccator to remove moisture, typically for laboratory testing, soil classification, and moisture content determination.
2 Determination of water content IS 2720 Part 2 : 1973

Water content:The determination of water content in soil involves measuring the mass loss of a soil sample after drying it in an oven at a temperature of 105°C ± 5°C to a constant mass.
3 Determination of Specific Gravity IS 2720 Part 3 : 1980

Specific Gravity:It is the ratio of the density of the soil solids to the density of water at a given temperature. According to IS 2720, specific gravity is determined using the pycnometer method or the density bottle method.
4 Grain size analysis IS 2720 Part 4 : 1985

Grain Size analysis:It refers to the proportion of different grain sizes present in a soil sample.According to Indian Standard 2720, grain size distribution can be determined using - Sieve Analysis: For coarse-grained soils and Hydrometer Analysis: For fine-grained soils
5 Determination of Liquid and Plastic limits IS 2720 Part 5 : 1985

Liquid Limit & Plastic Limit:The liquid limit is the moisture content at which the soil behaves like a liquid and flows when tilted, whereas plastic limit is the moisture content at which the soil behaves like a plastic and can be molded into a ball.
6 Determination of Shrinkage Factors IS 2720 Part 6 : 1972

Shrinkage Factor:It is the ratio of the volume of the soil in its natural state to the volume of the soil when it is completely dry. It is used to calculate the shrinkage limit, which is an important parameter in determining the soil's behavior and classification.
7 Determination of water content-dry density relation using light compaction IS 2720 Part 7 : 1980

Light Compaction:The objective of the light compaction test is to determine the maximum dry density and the optimum moisture content of the soil.
8 Determination of water content-dry density relation using heavy compaction IS 2720 Part 8 : 1983

Heavy Compaction:The objective of the heavy compaction test is to determine the maximum dry density and the optimum moisture content of the soil.
9 Determination of Unconfined Compressive Strength (UCS) IS 2720 Part 10 : 1991

UCS:Unconfined Compressive Strength (UCS) is the load per unit area required to cause a soil sample to fail in compression without lateral confinement.
10 Determination of the Shear Strength Parameters of a Specimen Tested in Unconsolidated Undrained Triaxial Compression Without the Measurement of Pore Water Pressure IS 2720 Part 11 : 1993

Unconsolidated Undrained Triaxial Compression: The test determines the undrained shear strength of saturated soils without confining pressure.
11 Direct shear test IS 2720 Part 13 : 1986

Direct Shear:The Direct Shear Test is a laboratory test used to determine the shear strength of a soil sample.
12 Determination of density index (relative density) of cohesionless soils IS 2720 Part 14 : 1983

Relative Density:It is the ratio of the difference between the in-situ density and the minimum density to the difference between the maximum density and the minimum density, expressed as a percentage.
13 Determination of Consolidation Properties IS 2720 Part 15 : 1986

Consolidation:Consolidation is the process by which a soil's volume decreases due to the expulsion of water from the soil's pores under an applied load, crucial for settlement predictions, soil stability, and design of foundations.
14 Laboratory determination of CBR IS 2720 Part 16 : 1987

California Bearing Ratio:CBR is a measure of the bearing capacity of a soil or aggregate material, used in pavement design, determining bearing capacity, and material classification.
15 Laboratory determination of Permeability IS 2720 Part 17 : 1986

PermeabilityPermeability is the measure of a soil's ability to allow water to flow through its pores, crucial for drainage, filtration, settlement, and stability analysis.
16 Determination of Free Swell Index of Soils IS 2720 Part 40 : 1977

Free Swell Index:Free Swell Index (FSI) is the ratio of the volume of a compacted soil sample after soaking in water to its initial dry volume.
17 Measurement of Swelling Pressure of Soils IS 2720 Part 41 : 1977

Swelling Pressure:It is the pressure exerted by a soil when it expands due to water absorption, crucial for foundation design, soil stability, pavement design, and geotechnical engineering projects.
